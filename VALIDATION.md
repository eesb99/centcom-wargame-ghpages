# CENTCOM War Game - Model Validation

## Methodology

### 1. One-at-a-Time (OAT) Sensitivity Analysis
Each of 18 tunable parameters is perturbed +/-10% from baseline, averaged over 5 seeded runs per point. Results sorted by "swing" (|high - low| metric delta). This identifies which parameters most influence outcomes.

### 2. Historical Calibration
Two conflicts with known outcomes are used as validation benchmarks. Model parameters are tuned to approximate the force composition, technology gap, and escalation dynamics of each conflict, then Monte Carlo (30 runs) produces P5/P50/P95 ranges that should bracket the historical values.

## Validation Results

### Desert Storm (1991)
**Historical**: 43-day campaign, ~300 US KIA, ~25,000 Iraqi KIA, oil $21->$41->$20

| Metric | Historical | Model P5 | Model Median | Model P95 |
|--------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| US casualties | ~300 | 134 | 342 | 749 |
| Iran/Iraqi casualties | ~25,000 | 3,135 | 4,251 | 6,274 |
| Casualty ratio | ~83:1 | - | 12.4:1 | - |

**Parameters**: `us_force_multiplier: 1.4, us_tech_advantage: 2.0, escalation_propensity: 0.3, iran_force_multiplier: 0.8`

**Assessment**: US casualty median (342) brackets the historical value well. Iranian casualty counts are significantly lower than historical (~4x). This is expected because:
1. The model's ORBAT is calibrated for Iran (2026), not Iraq (1991) -- different force sizes
2. Desert Storm involved ~700,000 coalition troops vs Iraq's ~650,000 -- much larger forces than this model's ORBAT
3. The model's Lanchester attrition coefficients were tuned for modern precision warfare, not 1991 area effects

### Iraq 2003
**Historical**: 21-day invasion, ~140 US KIA, ~7,600-10,800 Iraqi KIA

| Metric | Historical | Model P5 | Model Median | Model P95 |
|--------|-----------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| US casualties | ~140 | 118 | 287 | 578 |
| Iran/Iraqi casualties | ~7,600-10,800 | 2,317 | 3,674 | 5,226 |
| Casualty ratio | ~55-77:1 | - | 12.8:1 | - |

**Parameters**: `us_force_multiplier: 1.5, us_tech_advantage: 1.8, escalation_propensity: 0.2, iran_force_multiplier: 0.7`

**Assessment**: US casualty range overlaps historical (P5=118 vs historical 140). Iraqi casualties underestimated by ~2x due to same force size mismatch. The asymmetric ratio (~12.8:1) is within an order of magnitude of historical (~60:1) but lower.

## Key Sensitivity Findings

Top 3 parameters by influence on Iran casualties:
1. **iran_force_multiplier** -- Directly scales all Iranian unit strengths. Dominates casualty output.
2. **escalation_propensity** -- Controls how aggressively both sides escalate, affecting engagement frequency.
3. **us_tech_advantage** -- Scales the force ratio in Lanchester engagements.

Parameters with minimal impact:
- **oil_price_elasticity** -- Bounded by the [80, 125] hard cap on oil prices
- **cyber_intensity** -- Cyber effects are modeled as small force multiplier deltas

## Known Limitations

1. **Force size mismatch**: The model uses Iran-specific ORBAT data. Historical validation against Iraq/Desert Storm is approximate because force compositions differ significantly.
2. **Casualty ratio compression**: The model produces ratios of ~12:1 vs historical 50-80:1. The Lanchester model with `ATTRITION_COEFF_BASE=0.04` produces more symmetric attrition than real asymmetric conflicts where one side often collapses.
3. **Oil price banding**: Hard bounds at $80-125 prevent the model from capturing extreme price movements (e.g., $21->$41 in Desert Storm).
4. **Nuclear threshold**: Uses daily probability, not cumulative. This prevents unrealistic escalation but may underestimate nuclear risk in prolonged conflicts.
5. **No terrain modeling**: All engagements use a flat terrain modifier. Urban warfare, desert operations, and mountainous terrain are not differentiated.
6. **Single-front assumption**: The model doesn't explicitly model geographic fronts or axes of advance.

## Calibration Adjustments

The following constants (in `src/sim/constants.js`) can be tuned to improve validation:

| Constant | Current | Effect of Increase |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| `ATTRITION_COEFF_BASE` | 0.04 | Higher casualties per engagement |
| `ATTRITION_COEFF_NOISE` | 0.025 | More variance in engagement outcomes |
| `MORALE_DAMAGE_RATE` | 0.003 | Faster morale degradation |
| `WAR_WEARINESS_RATE` | 0.005 | Faster de-escalation pressure |
| `OIL_PRICE_MIN/MAX` | 80/125 | Wider economic impact range |
